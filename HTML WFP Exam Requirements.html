    MCAT

    The MCAT is the Medical College Admissions Test. It is a seven and a half hour exam that consists of four sections of multiple choice questions that cover the subjects listed below.

  • Biology (General)
  • Biochemistry
  • Chemistry (General and Organic)
  • Physics
  • Psychology
  • Sociology

    • The four sections are catagorized as Physiological, Social, and Biological Foundations of Behavior, Biological and Biochemical Foundations of Living Systems, Critical Analysis and Reasoning Skills, and Chemical and Physical FOudnations of Biological Systems. The highest score achievable is a 528. Each section tested is scored within a range of 118 to 132.

    GPA

    The average GPA for the accepted applicant for medical school is between 3.7 and 4.0 for most institutions.
